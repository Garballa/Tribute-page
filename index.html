Dr. Robert "Bob" Moog


Electronic music pioneer, innovator and inventor

Roger Powell and Bob Moog (right) with custom modular controller designed by Bob for Roger, at Radio City Music Hall, 1974.

Here's a time line of Dr. Moog's life:

  • 1934 - Born in New York City on May 23.
  • 1949 - Built his first theremin at age 14.
  • 1952 - Graduated from the Bronx high School of Science.
  • 1964 - Created the Moog synthesizer, and debuted the instrument in NY.
  • 1965 - Earned a PhD in engineering physics from Cornell University.
  • 1970 - Created the Minimoog, hailed as the most influential synth in history.
  • 1978 - Moved to NC and founded electronic instrument company, Big Briar.
  • 1993 - Research professor of music at the University of North Carolina at Asheville.
  • 2002 - renamed Big Briar to Moog Music after buying back the rights to the name.
  • 1984 - Consultant and vice president for new product research at Kurzweil Music Systems.
  • 2005 - Dies at age of 71.

"Everything has some consciousness, and we tap into that. It is about energy at its most basic level."
-Robert Moog
